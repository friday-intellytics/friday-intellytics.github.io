Thesis March 13, 2026

The Analytics Experience Needs a Rebuild

Think about how you interact with data at work. You open a dashboard. You wait for it to load. You squint at a chart, apply some filters, maybe export to a spreadsheet. If you are lucky, someone sends you a scheduled report every Monday. That interface slows Fast Insights before the first question even lands.

Now think about how you interact with everything else. Music, maps, shopping, messaging — every experience has been reimagined in the last decade. Analytics has not.

Frozen in 2005

The core paradigm of business analytics has not changed since the early days of BI: someone builds a dashboard, someone else looks at it. The tools have gotten prettier. The infrastructure has moved to the cloud. But the experience — the way a human actually interacts with data to make a decision — is fundamentally the same.

Dashboards are passive. Reports are static. Alerts are noisy. The analytics experience is a series of one-way broadcasts that hope the right person sees the right number at the right time.

The analytics experience is dead — and reinventing it is the need of the hour.

What AI-Assisted Analytics Changes

In an AI-native world, analytics is not something you go look at. It is something that comes to you, in context, at the moment you need it.

Imagine: instead of opening a dashboard, you ask a question in natural language and get a narrative answer with supporting data. Instead of a weekly report, you get a proactive alert that says "retention dropped in segment X because of Y — here are three options." Instead of a static chart, you get an interactive conversation with your data.

This is not science fiction. The building blocks exist today. What is missing is the design thinking — the willingness to throw out the dashboard paradigm and start from what a decision-maker actually needs.

Experience as Competitive Advantage

Companies that figure out this interface first will have a structural advantage. Their teams will make faster decisions, with better context, using less analyst time. That is the definition of Fast Insights.

The old experience served its era. But the era has changed. AI-Assisted Analytics is not just a new technology layer on top of the old experience. It is the catalyst for building an entirely new one, and for delivering Fast Insights through a better Analytics Experience.
