AI-Assisted Analytics Should Assist, Not Replace

Every wave of automation brings the same narrative: the machines will do the work, and the humans will step aside. In analytics, the current version of this story says AI will replace data analysts, data engineers, and eventually anyone who writes a SQL query.

We think this is wrong. Not because AI is not powerful enough, but because the framing misunderstands what analytics actually requires. The best Analytics Experience keeps experts in the driver's seat.

What AI Is Good At

AI excels at the mechanical layer of analytics work: writing boilerplate SQL, scanning thousands of rows for anomalies, generating chart variations, summarizing long datasets, and suggesting next questions. These are tasks that consume hours of an analyst's week and demand precision, not judgment.

When AI handles this layer, it does not replace the analyst. It frees them to do the work that actually matters — the work only a human can do.

What AI Cannot Do for the Analytics Experience

AI cannot tell you that the revenue spike last Tuesday was caused by a pricing error that the sales team is pretending did not happen. It cannot know that your CEO cares about retention more than growth this quarter. It cannot sense that the stakeholder asking for a dashboard actually needs a one-time answer to an urgent question.

The analyst provides judgment, context, and skepticism. AI provides speed. Together, you get fast insights without losing rigor.

AI-Assisted, Not AI-Automated

AI-assisted analytics means the human stays in the loop. The analyst is not a reviewer of AI output — they are the driver, with AI as a co-pilot that handles the tedious parts of the route.

This matters because analytics is not a production line. It is a conversation between data and decisions. Remove the human from that conversation and you get answers that are technically correct and practically useless.

The future of analytics is not fewer analysts. It is analysts who operate at ten times the speed, with AI handling the mechanics and humans handling the meaning. That is how teams get Fast Insights without hollowing out the Analytics Experience.
